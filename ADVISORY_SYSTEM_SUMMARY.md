## 🎯 Advisory Content by Section

### 2.1 Key Strengths and Process Integrity (9 questions)
- Focus: Assessment transparency, assessor access, process integrity
- Advice: Document improvements, provide unrestricted access, honest reporting
- Example: "Assessment integrity is crucial. Transparency is essential."

### 2.2 Learning & Consultation (1 question)
- Focus: Worker representation and consultation
- Advice: Hold formal consultations with worker representatives and unions
- Example: "ILO Convention 144 requires tripartite consultation."

### 2.3 Child Labour (6 questions)
- Focus: Age verification, hazardous work, young worker protection
- Advice: Strict government ID verification at hiring, document all checks
- Reminders: 🚨 CRITICAL - Child labour is prohibited and a crime

### 2.4 Forced Labour (13 questions)
- Focus: Voluntary employment, document handling, freedom of movement
- Advice: Never confiscate documents, ensure voluntary work, allow freedom
- Reminders: 🚨 CRITICAL - Document confiscation is human trafficking

### 2.5 Fair Recruitment (13 questions)
- Focus: Job advertising, recruitment fees, contract honesty
- Advice: Licensed agencies only, truthful ads, no worker fees
- Reminders: ⚠️ Deceptive recruitment is trafficking

### 2.6 Freedom of Association & Collective Bargaining (27 questions)
- Focus: Union rights, anti-retaliation, collective bargaining
- **8 Questions with Real-World Audit Findings:**
  - 2.6.2.1: Union independence (Yorkmars finding: union leaders assigned as managers)
  - 2.6.4.3: Employer interference (Yorkmars finding: automatic overtime to union members)
  - 2.6.4.6: Union manipulation (Yorkmars finding: union leaders promoted to manager positions)
- Reminders: ⚠️ CRITICAL - Anti-union retaliation is illegal

### 2.7 Compensation (43 questions)
- Focus: Minimum wage, deductions, benefits, overtime pay
- **2 Questions with Real-World Audit Findings:**
  - 2.7.2.1: Overtime wages (Yorkmars: machines restricted to limit work hours)
  - 2.10.1.4: Overtime limits (Yorkmars: artificial timing controls imposed)
- Reminders: 💰 Wage theft is the most common violation

### 2.8 Excessive Hours (10 questions)
- Focus: Work hour limits, mandatory overtime, scheduling
- Advice: Cap hours at legal limits, voluntary overtime only, advance scheduling
- Reminders: ⏰ Excessive hours harm productivity and worker health

### 2.9 Occupational Safety and Health (43 questions)
- Focus: Hazard elimination, PPE, emergency preparedness, safety training
- **2 Questions with Real-World Audit Findings:**
  - 2.9.9.7: Emergency exit locks (Yorkmars: door B 02 found locked during work)
  - 2.9.9.8: Escape routes (Yorkmars: exits blocked and obstructed)
- Reminders: 🚨 CRITICAL SAFETY - Prevention is non-negotiable

### 2.10 Working Time (34 questions)
- Focus: Regular hours, rest days, annual leave, breaks
- Advice: Hours ≤48/week, mandatory breaks, allow earned leave
- Reminders: ⏰ Rest is essential for safety and productivity

## 🔴 Critical Alert Indicators

- **🚨** = Life-threatening or crime-related (child labour, trafficking, locked exits)
- **💰** = Wage/compensation issues
- **⏰** = Working hours/rest day issues
- **⚖️** = Discrimination/equality issues
- **🤝** = Freedom of association/union issues
- **🌙** = Night work/shift issues
- **🧹** = Cleanliness/sanitation issues
- **🌍** = International standards
- **🚗** = Transportation/safety issues
- **💬** = Communication/consultation issues

## 🔄 Using the Advisory System

- Review advice for your compliance area
- Follow the practical steps listed
- Pay attention to critical reminders (🚨)

### For Auditors:
- Use reminders to identify high-risk areas
- Reference real audit findings when discussing non-compliance
- Leverage common problems section during interviews
- Use advice section to help factories develop remediation plans

### For Workers & Unions:
- Use advice to understand what compliance looks like

- Use audit findings to show this is a real, documented problem
